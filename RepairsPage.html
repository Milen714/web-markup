Get Rolling Again – Easy Steps to Fix Your Flat Tire!

Contact Us

Do it yourself and save!!!

Even the sturdy Dutch bikes, like the classic “Omafiets” and the trusty “Stadfiets,” can get a flat tire now and then. Luckily, fixing a flat is simpler than it seems and doesn’t require an expert! With just a basic puncture repair kit, you can learn to patch up your bike and avoid unnecessary trips to the bike shop. Here’s a step-by-step guide to get you back on two wheels in no time.

Follow these simple steps:

Get Ready – What You’ll Need:

You’ll need a standard tire repair kit (with tire levers, glue, and patches) and a bike pump. These kits are widely available at supermarkets and stores like “Action.”

Flip the Bike Over:

You can flip the bike over, so it's resting on the handlebars and seat, to work on the wheel. Then, you can remove the valve and retaining ring, and use the levers to take out the inner tube from the outer tire.

Locate the Puncture:

After inflating the tube, you can find the puncture by listening for air hissing out or by dipping the inflated tube in water to spot bubbles.

Prep and Patch the Puncture:

Once you find the puncture, roughen up the area with the sandpaper from the kit. Then, apply the glue, wait a bit, and stick on the patch. While the glue sets, carefully run your fingers around the outer tire to feel what caused the puncture and remove it carefully by pressing the sides of the tire.

Position the Tube and Tire:

Once done exracting the little puncture culprit and the glue is set (about 5 minutes) feed the valve stem through the rim hole and partially inflate the inner tube to give it a circular shape. This will make it easier to fit back into the outer tire.

Roll the Tire Onto the Rim:

Start from the valve location and use your thumbs to carefully roll the tire, along with the inner tube, back onto the rim. When you reach the last section, use a tire lever if needed, ensuring the tube isn’t pinched.

Inflate and Get Back Riding:

Fully inflate the tire, checking that the tire sits evenly around the rim. Once properly inflated, your bike is ready to hit the road again!
