Let’s get Shopping!!!

(But before that: )

Cycling in Amsterdam is as regular as eating or breathing. This makes the bike the most essential tool to get you where you need to go. The process of buying a bicycle can be a minefield for the uninitiated. Having the correct information is critical in avoiding buying junk and spending money on subsequent repairs. Used bicycles tend to offer the most value for the money compared to cheap new bikes. They also provide peace of mind since they look used, a common deterrent against thieves primarily interested in flashy new bikes. You don’t have to worry about scratching it since the rule on the streets here is the more scuffed your ride looks, the better.

Buying New Second Hand

If you're in the market for a used bicycle, there are several productive ways to find one: Firstly, it's important to be mindful of potential pitfalls when buying a second-hand bike. While purchasing a used bike can be a cost-effective option, it's crucial to avoid dealing with untrustworthy sellers. Be cautious of individuals who approach you with offers for brand new bikes at unrealistically low prices, as these may be stolen goods. Buying a stolen bike can lead to serious legal consequences. The same level of vigilance applies when browsing online marketplaces. If a seller is offering multiple bikes for unusually low prices and does not operate as a registered business, there's a chance they are involved with stolen goods. Even when dealing with a legitimate seller, it's vital to thoroughly inspect the bike's condition before making a purchase. Take the time to test it out to ensure it meets your standards before making any financial commitments.

Second hand:

Facebook Groups:

Joining specific Facebook groups such as Expat Republic Amsterdam, Buy & Sell Amsterdam, and Gratis Ophalen Amsterdam can be a proactive and secure approach to finding a used bike. Sellers in these groups often part with their old bikes for reasons such as upgrading or relocating, rather than selling stolen items.

Markplaats:

Marktplaats, the local equivalent of Craigslist, is another proactive avenue for locating a used bike. Its filtering options allow you to narrow your search to a specific region. By using keywords like "fietsen" or "fiets" (Dutch for "bicycle"), you can streamline your search. In Amsterdam, the typical bicycles are referred to as "omafiets," or "grandma's bicycles" - they are affordable, comfortable, and durable.

Local markets/Flea markets:

Exploring local markets such as Waterlooplein Market near the city center and Amsterdam’s largest market, Albert Cuyp Market, offers a productive way to browse a wide selection of old bikes and accessories in a single location.

Local bike shops:

Although slightly pricier than other options, local bike shops are a constructive and dependable venue for purchasing a used bike. Buying from a registered business ensures that the bike is in excellent, safe-to-ride condition. Certain shops even provide a warranty for a few months after purchase and may offer a buyback option if you decide to upgrade.

Ask around, Don’t be Shy:

If you're having trouble finding an affordable bike, why not ask your colleagues or new Dutch friends if they have an extra bike they'd be willing to sell? Chances are you'll have success with this approach, especially considering that you now live in a city with more bikes than people, statistically speaking.

Buying New:

Online retailers:

If you're in the market for a new bike, online retailers like Bol.com and physical stores like DECATHLON have great budget-friendly options for bikes of all types and sizes.

The Elite Way!

But let's say and you feel warm and it’s not from your morning oat milk latte but the extra € 2.5k burning a hole in your pocket. Then, the hip Dutch brand Vanmoof is what you are looking for. From them, you can get an electric bike with a streamlined design, equipped with an alarm system so loud that it will make every thief avoid your bike. And if it goes missing, the company provides the service of their bike hunters to locate your bike through GPS and bring it back home to you.

Bike retailers:

If you have a more flexible budget and are in search of a new high-quality bicycle, consider visiting a reputable bike retail store, either in person or online. These establishments can assist you in exploring a variety of bike sizes and styles that align with your preferences. Notable options include Mantel.com, which operates exclusively online due to the absence of physical locations in Amsterdam, and "Het Zwarte Fietsenplan".
